Section 01

Welcome to the Committee

Welcome to the Planning Committee. You are not just volunteers helping with an event. You are founding builders of a platform designed to help secondary school graduates and university students transition intentionally into academics, skills, and future opportunities.

This conference exists because many students move through education without structure, clarity, or guidance. Our mission is to change that.

Every decision you make, every message you send, every design you create contributes to shaping students' futures. You are part of something at its beginning. Most students attend events. Very few build movements.

This conference may start small — but its impact depends on the seriousness, creativity, and ownership each of you brings. If we execute well, we are not just hosting a program — we are building a system that helps students think differently about learning, growth, and their future.

Thank you for choosing to build with us. Let's create something meaningful.

Section 02

About the Conference

🎯

Conference Purpose

The Student Transition Conference is a 5-day online academic workshop designed to move students from:

  • Confusion → Clarity
  • Passive schooling → Intentional learning
  • Academic survival → Future readiness
👥

Target Audience

  • Final-year secondary school students (SS3)
  • JAMB candidates (Jambites)
  • Fresh university students (100 Level)

Note: SS3 and Jambite recruitment via school partnerships, exam prep communities, and tutoring platforms.

📅

Event Format

  • Duration: 5 days, fully online
  • Daily sessions: ~1 hour per day
  • Platform: Zoom / Google Meet (TBC)
  • Includes speaker-led + host-led sessions
  • Interactive learning + community engagement
💡

Conference Vision

To help secondary school and early university students transition into independent, strategic academic thinkers through structured learning, systems thinking, and mentorship.

Theme: "Beyond Admission: The Transition Blueprint"

Section 07

Planning Timeline & Phases

Every team operates within these five planning phases. Your lead will assign specific milestones within each phase.

Phase 1 — Foundation
Structure, Roles & Branding
Confirm committee roles, build brand identity system (logo, colors, type), finalize conference pillars and academic structure. Set up shared Google Drive and communication channels.
Phase 2 — Build
Website, Content & Speaker Outreach
Launch registration website with payment integration and automation. Begin speaker outreach and confirmations. Develop marketing materials and curriculum outlines.
Phase 3 — Launch
Marketing, Sales & Community
Activate full marketing campaign. Open registration. Build WhatsApp community and begin participant onboarding. Finalize all speaker briefing kits and session content.
Phase 4 — Execution
The 5-Day Conference
Run daily sessions, manage technical execution, moderate community, collect daily feedback, ensure smooth timing and a premium participant experience across all five days.
Phase 5 — Post-Event
Wrap-Up, Impact & Alumni
Distribute certificates, deliver speaker appreciation packages, compile impact reports, publish testimonials, onboard alumni community, and document lessons for the next edition.
Section 08

General Workflow Expectations

Every committee member is expected to operate at a professional organizational standard — not as a casual volunteer. Below are the non-negotiable behavioral expectations.

📅
Attend All Meetings

Be present for all scheduled meetings. Absence must be communicated 24hrs in advance with a reason and a delegate.

⏰
Meet Deadlines — Always

Deadlines are real commitments. If you cannot meet one, raise it 48hrs before — never after it passes.

💬
Communicate Proactively

Silence creates failure. Update your lead regularly — weekly minimum, more during active phases.

📁
Document Everything

Nothing should exist only in private chats. All work, decisions, and updates go into the shared Google Drive and Notion workspace.

🤝
Collaborate Across Teams

Your team doesn't exist in isolation. Branding works with Tech. Academic works with Speakers. Build bridges.

🎓
Student First, Always

Every decision must ask: "Does this improve the participant's experience?" If not, reconsider it.

Section 09

Core Operating Rules

01

Respect Time

Deadlines are binding. Treat every due date as a professional commitment. Lateness without notice is unacceptable.

02

Communicate Early

Raise problems immediately — not after they become emergencies. No hero moves. Collective awareness prevents failure.

03

One Decision Authority

Every team has one lead who finalizes team decisions. Escalate to founders only when leads cannot resolve.

04

Document Everything

If it's not in the Drive or Notion, it doesn't exist officially. All work, decisions, meeting notes, and progress must be saved to both platforms.

05

Student Experience First

Every decision must improve participant value. This is our north star. It supersedes convenience or speed.

06

Professional Conduct

We operate like an organization, not a casual group. Your tone, output, and follow-through reflects on the brand.

07

No Last-Minute Chaos

Preparation prevents emergencies. Every crisis in planning is caused by something that could have been done earlier.

08

Respect Your Team Lead

Leads are accountable for their teams. Support your lead by being reliable, transparent, and solution-focused.

Section 10

Communication Structure

💬

Main Planning Group

All committee leads in one WhatsApp/Slack group. No side conversations on critical decisions.

🗂️

Team Working Groups

Each team has its own private working group for task management and daily coordination.

📹

Weekly Leadership Meeting

Leads only. Every team reports progress, blockers, and next steps. Mandatory attendance.

📁

Google Drive & Notion Workspace

Two central hubs for all assets: Google Drive (files, graphics, recordings) and Notion (planning docs, task tracking, team wikis). Every committee member has access to both.

🔔

Escalation Protocol

Issue → Team Lead → Lead resolves or escalates to Founders within 24hrs. No silent failures.

Response Time Expectation: All messages in planning groups must receive a response within 6 hours during active planning phases. During conference week, response time drops to 1 hour maximum.
